Overview

As NPRâ€™s in-house technology research & development center, NPR Labs provides a variety of tools to assist public broadcasters nationwide. Its Mapping And Population System (MAPS) is an interactive web application that provides terrain-sensitive coverage maps for reception of all public radio and television stations in the U.S.

During my internship in the spring of 2017, I worked as part of a small team to build a new version of the tool from the ground up.

Role: UI/UX Designer

Team: Christian Wood (Developer), Alice Goldfarb (Manager)

Before

Objective

Project Needs

Last updated in 2011, the MAPS tool was in need of a dramatic overhaul to both its underlying data and its core functionality. This included updating census and FCC coverage data, giving users the ability to view FCC contours in addition to signal coverages, and providing more detailed station information.

For my part, a primary challenge was to redesign the updated version to be responsive across devices.

Significance

The maps are designed to guide station-planning efforts to improve reception and develop new services, including new FM and TV stations and translators, as well as HD radio and mobile TV.

With an outdated interface and similarly outdated information, the map provided little of its intended utility nowadays. A new version with accurate broadcast and demographic information could assist local broadcasters in making technology and programming decisions, while providing NPR with an overview of its affiliated local stations and the greater public media landscape.

The project took on additional importance as the future of public broadcasting was being debated by legislators just blocks away from NPR headquarters in the Capitol, as public media advocates could utilize the tool to inform public policy development.

Process

As with any redesign, the process began with an assessment of the current version. Working through the key user flows, I flagged potential points of friction and identified opportunities for improvement.
